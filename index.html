QUICK START GUIDE

How StudySpark works

Everything you need to go from notes to a confident test day.

01

Create your account

Enter your name, email, grade, and main subjects. Your information is stored only in this browser.

02

Add your notes

Open My Notes, choose a subject, and paste or type your class material. Save or analyze it for key ideas.

03

Generate flashcards

Choose a subject and generate cards. Flip each card, then mark it “Know it” or “Need review.”

04

Take a quiz

Create a five-question quiz from a subject. Submit your answers to see a score and clear explanations.

05

Build a study plan

Add your test date, topics, and available minutes. StudySpark creates a daily sequence of focused tasks.

06

Track your progress

Every saved note, reviewed card, and quiz score updates your progress dashboard automatically.

07

How to Organize With Folders

Go to Dashboard and click “Create New Folder.” Name your folder and choose a color. Add notes, flashcards, quizzes, or study plans to it. Click the folder card to open it, or use its three-dot menu to rename, change color, move, or delete it. When deleting, you can move items to No folder or permanently delete the contents. New users start without custom folders and create their own.

08

How to Update Your Courses

Open Account Settings, select or remove courses, then click Save courses. To create your own class, click “+ Add Custom Course,” enter a unique name up to 30 characters, and click Add. Custom courses appear immediately across notes, files, flashcards, quizzes, plans, and the dashboard. You can rename or delete custom courses later.
